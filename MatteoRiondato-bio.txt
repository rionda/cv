Matteo Riondato is an associate professor of computer science at Amherst
College, and a visiting faculty at Brown University. Previously, he was a
research scientist at Two Sigma, and a postdoc at Stanford. His research focuses
on algorithms for knowledge discovery, data mining, and machine learning: he
develops methods to analyze rich datasets, including graphs and time series, as
fast as possible and in a statistically sound way. He received an NSF CAREER
award in 2023, and the 2025 SDM/IBM Early Career Data Mining Research Award. His
works were recognized with best-of-conference at multiple data mining venues.
